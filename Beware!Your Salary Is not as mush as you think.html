Beware! Your salary is not as much as you think .

We’re all familiar with the word CTC. Every year, companies come to colleges to recruit graduates for a variety of different positions; dangling the golden egg of ‘CTC’ over our heads. But do you really get the entirety of the promised amount to use? To answer that question, let’s dig a little bit further into what CTC means.
 

CTC stands for ‘Cost to Company'. Colloquially, it is the cost that an employer bears for hiring and maintaining its employees.

The CTC includes:

1. Basic Salary - Amount payable to the employee for their services ( subject to taxes).

2. Allowance:

  • House Rent Allowance
  • Dearness Allowance
  • Medical Allowance
  • Others( As per company policies)
    • Provident Fund(A retirement savings plan to which employees contribute a portion of their salary)
    • Income Tax
    • Professional Tax

  1. If an employee's salary is ₹40,000/month and the company pays an additional ₹5,000 for health insurance, the CTC is ₹45,000/month. Employees do not directly receive the CTC amount as cash.

    CTC = Basic Salary + Allowances + Deductibles

    The In-hand salary is the amount deposited into your account every month. It is the amount received after tax and other deductions.
