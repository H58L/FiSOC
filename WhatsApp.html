How does WhatsApp earn?
 (1.5 min read)

India has more WhatsApp users than any other country, with 390.1 million monthly active users. Worldwide, more than 100 billion messages are being exchanged on its platform every single day.

With no advertisements and no plans to implement them in the future, from where does WhatsApp make its money?

In its initial days, WhatsApp relied on the freemium business model — the product was free for a year to lure users and subsequently charged a small yearly subscription fee for continued service.
 

Does WhatsApp sell your data?

Your data is the currency that free services like Instagram and YouTube use to gain revenue.

As of now, Meta (WhatsApp’s parent company) claims that it does not use data from WhatsApp to optimize its Ad services. However, some amount of data is still shared with Meta.

The only way to make sure that your data is not being used is by deleting your WhatsApp account (which translates to giving up your social and professional life).

So how exactly does it earn?

       1.WhatsApp For Business -
  • The WhatsApp Business app provides a host of tools for businesses to access company insights making it an attractive tool for businesses.
  • Just a month after the launch of the WhatsApp Business API, big tech companies like Netflix, Uber, and others had already begun testing the service. 
 
       2. WhatsApp pay -
  • In November 2020, it enabled peer-to-peer payments in India.
  • Luckily, sending payments to others remains free of charge. WhatsApp monetizes its payment feature on the business side by charging a small transaction fee.

The app has a growing revenue stream with endless possibilities that will allow it to bring in more income over time.
 

Fun Fact -
Before starting WhatsApp, The co-founders applied to work at Facebook, only to be rejected. Ironically, in 2009 Meta bought their company for a whopping $19 billion!
