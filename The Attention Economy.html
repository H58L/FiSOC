Debts are associated with negative connotations and are commonly tied to bankruptcy. Debt is something borrowed by one party from another. They allow our economy to function as it does, and people or organisations to make purchases that they cannot currently afford. In short, debt is a necessary evil.

Hence, it is important to note the difference between good and bad debt. Good debt helps increase your wealth and enhance your life, whereas bad debt would involve borrowing money purely for consumption or to purchase rapidly depleting assets.


Here is what the current debt system looks like:

  • Debts have to be paid back by deadlines that are usually at rigid, regular intervals. Not abiding by these could have consequences varying from small fines to seizing the borrower’s collateral.
  • Most varieties of debt have some percentage of interest that would have to be paid. Unsecured debts, such as personal loans, have higher rates of interest
  • Secured debts, though they have lower interest rates, require the borrower to provide an asset that can be seized by the lender as collateral.
  • There are hidden charges associated with most debts, such as processing fees, fines, transactional fees, charges on prepayment, etc.
  
An ideal system would probably have some of the following features:
  • Little to no interest rates.
  • Flexible and considerate time constraints for the money to be paid back.
  • Negating the need for a property to be given as collateral for security.
  • Open and transparent process of all costs involved from the start.

However, the ideal system is only designed to benefit the borrower. Financial instruments such as interest rates, collateral, regular payments, and screening are a form of security for lenders.

 

Some examples of debt are:

  • Mortgages:   Used where money is required to buy or maintain any real estate. These loans have a much longer repayment term(usually over a decade) and lower interest rates since a larger sum of money and collateral are involved.
  • Student loans:  Catered to individuals with little to no income, hence there is usually a grace period until the repayment of the loan starts, typically when they start working. However, these terms vary from lender to lender, and interest rates are higher than those of mortgages.
  • Personal loans:  Specific to certain situations (like wedding loans, medical loans, family loans, etc) and usually lend less money than mortgages with no collateral, but higher interest rates.
  • Debt consolidation loans:  A form of personal loan, but it is worth noting that, unlike other loans that will not be granted if the financial status of the borrower is not secure. These loans are specifically given to borrowers in debt. In cases where there are multiple debts that a borrower is buried in, they may consolidate all their debts into one.

An increase in short-term, well-invested debt, especially in the public sector, helps increase aggregate demand and productivity, and in turn, employment and investments in a country. However, if the debt is not repaid on time it can have adverse effects. Maintaining a healthy balance is essential for economic growth.
