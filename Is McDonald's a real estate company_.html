What makes McDonald’s a real estate company? 
 
McDonald’s has more than 38,000 outlets in more than 100 countries and around 75 burgers are sold every second !!

But their McBurgers aren’t the reason why the company generates multi-billion dollar profits.
The real money-maker? Real estate.

Do you think  McDonald’s owns all these outlets?
No, They do not.

Approximately 93% Of McDonald’s restaurants worldwide are owned and operated by independent local business owners. McDonald’s leases out property to these owners. These owners are responsible for all the costs of running the restaurant while also paying McDonald’s rent, and a monthly service fee (royalties) equal to 4% of gross sales.
This is what is called a Franchise Business Model.

Franchisor :

The parent company (here, McDonald’s corporation) that sells the rights to use their brand to franchisees.

Franchisee :

The individual or company who buys the rights to sell the products or services of a pre-established business.
In conclusion, even if people stop eating burgers tomorrow, McDonald’s is not going down. It will still be supported by its massive real estate empire. That’s how a sustainable business model is built.
