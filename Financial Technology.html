EMPOWERING FINANCIALLY


To serve these markets, you have to re-imagine how money can be managed and moved because there’s going to be more change in the next five years in financial services than in the past 30.
- Dan Schulman, CEO, PayPal

 

These are exciting times we’re in. The world is fast-paced and constantly changing. The rate at which technology is growing is unprecedented. But what does this mean for one of the leading industries in the fifth-largest economy of the world?  We are entering a new era in finance.


Financial Technology or Fin-Tech refers to the integration of technology into financial services. Fin-Tech includes anything from payment applications like Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm, etc. to complex blockchain networks. covering encrypted transactions.

A brief timeline :

1950s
Credit cards became one of the first fintech products available to the public.

1970s
The establishment of NASDAQ, the world’s first digital stock exchange took shape.

2008-2009
By the beginning of the 21st century, all banks’ internal processes were fully digitalized.

With the emergence of the global financial crisis of 2008, the general public developed a distrust of the traditional banking system. This led to the release of Bitcoin v0.1 in 2009, which was soon followed by the boom of different cryptocurrencies.

As always, everything that can become more efficient will become so eventually. You might have frequently come across “Web 3.0” ?

What exactly is Web 3.0 in finance and why is it so important ?

Web 3.0 is the concept of the next generation of the web, in which most users will be connected via a decentralized network and can retain ownership of their data.

In 2022, the growth of FinTech was dominated by Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, Blockchain Technology, and Big Science & Big Data. Let’s explore these trends in detail:

  • Artificial Intelligence (AI): AI is being used to analyze investment opportunities, optimize portfolios, and mitigate risks amongst many other functions.

  • Machine Learning (ML): Machine Learning uses computer algorithms and analytics, which can be used in the fintech industry to build predictive models that can solve business problems.

  • Blockchain Technology: A blockchain is a type of shared database that creates a permanent record of transactions. It allows all the participants in a transaction to see the same data at the same time. Many view the blockchain as immutable or immune to manipulation. It is applied in cryptocurrencies, real estate and payment processing

  • Data Science & Big Data: The term Big data refers to alternative data sources that can be used to analyze even unstructured data (such as text, images, and spoken languages) on a large scale. This data is so large and complex that it becomes difficult to process using traditional methods. Big Data marketing is now already being embraced by marketers and giant conglomerates like Netflix, Amazon, Kroger, and Airbnb.
     

The Indian fintech ecosystem is setting global benchmarks, with thriving innovations, digitally savvy banks, and market-driven open-banking frameworks. The 2023 budget has further provided support for the ecosystem, allowing companies like Paytm, RazorPay, MobiKwick, CRED, BharatPe, PolicyBazaar, Yelo, etc to flourish.

It is safe to say that FinTech is an integral part of our lives now and its influence will only grow.
